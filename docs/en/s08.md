# Admin Settings and Access Control

> "Role design and permission control — get this right once and operations run themselves."

## The Problem

Two failure modes exist in poorly configured workspaces.

Too permissive: everyone can see and change everything, creating security risks and accidental overwrites.

Too restrictive: team members can't do their jobs without constantly asking an admin for help.

As organizations grow, the cost of poorly designed roles compounds.

A teammate with the wrong role either blocks work or creates risk.

## The Solution

Design roles and memberships based on the principle of least privilege — give each person exactly what they need to do their job, nothing more.

DevRev's role system makes this achievable without constant maintenance overhead.

## How It Works

**System Roles**

DevRev includes three built-in system roles that cannot be modified.

| Role | What They Can Do |
|------|-----------------|
| Owner | Full workspace control including billing and all settings |
| Admin | Manage workspace settings, invite members, assign roles |
| Member | Create and update tickets, issues, and comments. No settings access |

For most organizations, these three roles cover 80% of cases.

**Custom Roles**

When system roles are not granular enough, custom roles fill the gap. Common examples:

- **Support Lead**: Can manage tickets and SLA policies, but cannot access engineering settings
- **External Partner**: Read-only access limited to specific Parts

Custom roles are defined by selecting specific permissions from DevRev's permission catalog.

**Groups**

Managing permissions per individual is expensive at scale. Groups solve this by letting you assign roles to collections of users.

When someone joins the support team, add them to the Support Group — their permissions are set automatically. When someone moves to a different team, update their group membership in one place.

**Key Workspace Settings**

| Settings Category | What to Configure |
|-------------------|------------------|
| General | Workspace name, timezone, default language |
| Members | Invite users, assign roles, manage groups |
| Parts | Define product hierarchy |
| SLA Policies | Response and resolution targets by priority |
| Integrations | External service connections |

## What You Will Learn

**Before this session:**
- Everyone has Admin access because it was the path of least resistance
- Role design has never been formally considered
- Member management is handled case by case

**After this session:**
- You can explain the difference between Owner, Admin, and Member roles
- You can design custom roles suited to your organization's structure
- You understand how groups reduce long-term administration overhead
