# Using Analytics Dashboards

> "One dashboard for support, engineering, and operations — because your KPIs don't live in silos."

## The Problem

Your support KPIs are in Zendesk.

Your engineering metrics are in Jira.

Your customer health data is in a spreadsheet someone updates manually every Friday.

Getting a complete picture requires exporting CSVs, joining them in Excel, and hoping nothing is stale by the time the report reaches anyone who can act on it.

## The Solution

Because DevRev stores support, engineering, and customer data in a unified platform, its analytics can span all three without data pipelines or manual exports.

Build dashboards once and they stay current automatically.

**The AI Trifecta**

DevRev's analytics layer is one of three core intelligence engines — alongside Search and Workflows — that form what the platform calls the AI Trifecta. Analytics in DevRev supports SQL queries, time-series data, and RLHF-based analysis, which enables dashboards that do more than display counts.

## How It Works

**Built-in Reports**

DevRev provides built-in report types covering the most common operational needs.

| Report Type | What It Shows |
|-------------|--------------|
| Ticket Volume | Count of tickets created, resolved, and open over time |
| SLA Compliance | Percentage of tickets meeting response and resolution targets |
| First Response Time | Average time to first response by priority, team, or product area |
| Issue Backlog | Open issues by priority, part, and sprint |
| Customer Health | Activity and sentiment trends for Rev Users |

**Custom Dashboards**

Beyond built-in reports, you can compose custom dashboards by selecting metrics, applying filters, and arranging widgets.

Common filter dimensions:
- Date range
- Priority (Blocker / High / Medium / Low)
- Part (product area)
- Assignee or team
- Ticket type or issue type

**Saved Views**

Dashboards can be saved and shared with teammates. A support team lead might maintain a daily operations view; an engineering manager might maintain a sprint health view. Both live in DevRev and reflect the same underlying data.

**Connecting Support and Engineering Metrics**

Because tickets and issues share the same data model, you can answer cross-functional questions directly in DevRev:

- Which Parts generate the most support tickets?
- What is the average time from ticket creation to linked issue resolution?
- Which issues, when resolved, would eliminate the most recurring tickets?

These questions typically require data joins across separate tools. In DevRev, they are standard queries.

## What You Will Learn

**Before this session:**
- KPIs are scattered across multiple tools
- Reporting requires manual data export and assembly
- Cross-functional metrics (support + engineering) are not regularly tracked

**After this session:**
- You can build a custom dashboard with ticket volume and SLA compliance widgets
- You understand how to filter metrics by Part, priority, and date range
- You can frame cross-functional questions that DevRev can answer without data exports
