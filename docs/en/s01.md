# What Is DevRev: The AI-Native Work Platform

> "One work platform that connects builders and users — answers and controls grounded in the same data model. Understand the world DevRev is building."

## The Problem

AI is already useful for chat and summarization. But in day-to-day work, you also need answers that are **correct across systems of record**, respect **permissions**, remain **auditable**, and keep **execution** under control when actions are taken. Without that foundation, responses can sound plausible yet still be hard to trust or operate on at scale (this chapter is **not** Claude-specific).

### Tool fragmentation

When support (e.g. Zendesk), development (e.g. Jira), and AI (ChatGPT, Copilot, Claude, etc.) live in separate products, you often get:

- Customer signal does not reach engineering / ticket → issue is slow
- Repeat reports go unnoticed  
→ **Tool silos become team silos.**

### Limits of general assistants and “wiring”

Assistants excel at chat and summarization, but **facts across SoRs, who may see what, and evidence after the fact** are usually a separate design — hence the gap between **plausible** and **business-correct** answers.

**Claude Code**, **Claude Cowork**, and similar options push the front-end forward. **MCP** and **Skills** expand what you can wire — yet with a weak data layer you still face **permission drift, chained-call cost, and operational complexity**.

### Probabilistic LLMs vs. what enterprises need

- **LLM + RAG** stays **probabilistic** at the core. For decisions that must hold in context, you need relationships as data — typically a **knowledge graph** — not only retrieval scores.
- **RBAC** and **coherent rollback after actions** (reverting changes across multiple records/objects with auditability) remain hard with **a general model plus connectors alone**.
- In practice, you also need to connect **structured data** (CRM, tickets, issues) with **unstructured data** (emails, call logs, chats, specs) so you can trace “which customer / product / event” each artifact belongs to under permissions. Retrieving loose snippets is not enough; the links themselves must be correct and auditable.

## The Solution

DevRev unifies support (Rev), development (Dev), and AI into a single SaaS platform.

The point is not “rip and replace your tools and operations,” but to keep your existing SoRs (CRM, ticketing, dev tracking, etc.) and add a foundation that binds context under permissions and evidence.

DevRev has three main applications:

- **Support App**: Handles ticket management, SLA policies, and customer interactions
- **Build App**: Manages the development cycle including issues, sprints, and releases
- **Observe App**: Provides session replay and funnel analysis for customer behavior visibility

Additionally, PLuG is an embeddable live chat widget that brings customer touchpoints directly into the platform.

The official brand name for the full platform is **Computer by DevRev** (pair this mentally with the "**Computer**" AI teammate described under How It Works).

## How It Works

DevRev's design philosophy is "AI-native." AI was not bolted on after the fact; it is built into the platform's foundation.

DevRev now brands the entire platform as **Computer by DevRev** — positioned as "an operating system for both human and AI agents." This is not a feature name; the platform itself is **Computer**.

The key is not only “use a smarter model,” but whether you have a data layer that binds **records, permissions, and evidence** in a reusable way. As models evolve, those enterprise requirements do not disappear; without that foundation, correctness and controls across SoRs remain fragile.

At the core is an AI teammate called "**Computer**." The platform emphasizes enterprise-facing values such as the following (governance and execution details also appear in [s14](/en/s14)):

- **Precise Search / Answers**: Compose context from the right records and fields so what you pass to reasoning stays high quality
- **Permission-aware Memory**: Use the knowledge graph’s relationships under **who may see what**
- **Auditability and explainability**: Aim to keep conclusions traceable to records wherever feasible
- **Separation of workflows and actions**: Keep verifiable answers distinct from execution and automation so controls are easier to reason about

Examples close to the user include:

### Fetch-style vs. Memory (at a glance)

| Focus | **Fetch-style** (model + API/MCP) | **Computer Memory** |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Relationships | Rediscover / rebuild each session | Read **typed edges**; joins/filters **deterministic** |
| Cost | Tokens and time compound | Tends to scale with **result sets** (see [public comparison](/en/memory-vs-fetch-ai-accuracy-and-cost)) |

- Automatic ticket classification and triage
- Summarizing a customer's past inquiry history
- Detecting similar tickets and suggesting related issues

The bullets above are **examples** of what **Computer** does close to the user. Next, grasp the **platform map**: Computer is built on **four foundations** (you only need the names and roles here; deeper design and Agent Studio details appear in [s14](/en/s14)).

**The four foundations of Computer by DevRev**

Computer is not a single AI feature — it is built on four foundations.

| Foundation | Role | Examples |
|------------|------|----------|
| **AirSync** | Bidirectional sync with 50+ external tools | Bring Jira, Zendesk, Salesforce, and more into DevRev in near real time and manage them as unified objects |
| **Memory** | Patented AI-native knowledge graph | Stores relationships among customers, products, tickets, and issues so search and agents can use them **under viewing permissions** |
| **Foundational Services** | Shared platform for search, automation, and analytics | Full-text search, workflows, and dashboards across the platform — a shared baseline for **reuse and explainability** |
| **Agent Studio** | No-code environment for AI agents | Define goals, tools, and **constraints** for autonomous agents (execution details in [s14](/en/s14)) |

Together, these foundations power the concrete behaviors above — triage, knowledge retrieval, and more. AirSync aggregates external data; Memory structures it; Foundational Services provide processing; Agent Studio ties them into autonomous agents.

Here is how traditional tools map to DevRev:

| Traditional Tool | DevRev Equivalent |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Zendesk / Freshdesk | Support App |
| Jira / Linear | Build App |
| Hotjar / FullStory | Observe App |
| Intercom / Drift | PLuG |
| ChatGPT / Copilot / Claude (manual integration) | Computer (built-in platform AI) |

The ChatGPT / Copilot / Claude → Computer row is **not** about ditching general assistants or swapping in a “smarter model.” General assistants are excellent at conversation, summarization, and externalized thinking — and with Claude Code, MCP, and skills you can cover more ground. Yet LLMs stay probabilistic, RAG can miss or noise, and requirements like **RBAC** and **coherent rollback after actions** are hard to satisfy with **model + connectors alone**. **Computer by DevRev** is a **full stack** on one data model — Memory as a knowledge graph plus shared services — so **each new model generation** can receive **meaningful** internal context under controls, not just a longer prompt.

The key point is that these are not separate products. They operate on the same data model. Support tickets and development issues share the same platform, structurally eliminating information silos between teams.

## What You Will Learn

**Before this session:**
- You have heard of DevRev but do not know what it specifically does
- You are dissatisfied with Jira + Zendesk but lack criteria to evaluate alternatives
- "AI-native" is a vague buzzword
- You use ChatGPT, Copilot, Claude, or similar assistants at work but worry about evidence and permissions across SoRs

- You sense that “LLM + RAG stays plausible,” and that RBAC or system-wide rollback is more than an assistant alone can guarantee

**After this session:**

- You can describe how Computer keeps internal knowledge graph-connected so new model generations still get meaningful, policy-aligned context
